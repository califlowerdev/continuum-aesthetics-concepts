Meet your physician

Emergency room precision.
Aesthetic restraint.

Dr. Adam Brunson, MD is a board-certified emergency physician who chose aesthetic medicine as his second specialty. The combination is the whole point.

The story

Trained where
precision is survival

Dr. Brunson earned his MD at the University of South Carolina School of Medicine, then completed his emergency medicine residency at Jackson Memorial Hospital with the University of Miami, one of the busiest trauma centers in the country. He is board certified by the American Board of Emergency Medicine and holds active staff privileges at South Miami Hospital.

Emergency medicine teaches two things aesthetic medicine badly needs: deep working knowledge of anatomy under pressure, and judgment about when not to act. He began practicing aesthetic medicine in 2021 and opened the Brickell studio to give that work a permanent home.

His patients tend to say the same three things: he takes his time, he never pushes more than you need, and nobody can tell you had anything done. That is the brief he works to.

The Continuum Method

How every plan is built

I.

Assess

A physician consultation first, always. Anatomy, goals, history and, where relevant, labs. No treatment is booked before this conversation.

II.

Plan

A mapped, personalized plan with conservative dosing as the default. You will know what is being done and why before anything happens.

III.

Treat

Performed methodically by a physician. Patients describe the process the way one review put it: mapped, precise, methodical.

IV.

Maintain

Follow-up, monitoring for wellness therapies, and direct access for questions after treatment. Results are managed over time, not per visit.
